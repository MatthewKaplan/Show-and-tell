Chautauqua Trail

Chautauqua Park

Starting from the main trailhead at Chautauqua, this trail ascends towards the majestic flatirons. Try to peel your eyes away from these awe-inspiring geologic formations enough to watch your step. This is one of the most used trails in the whole park so it is wide and mostly smooth. As you gain elevation, take a look back to the east and admire Boulder from above.

This trail runs straight through the heart of Chautauqua meadows. Due to the proximity of the parking lot and other infrastructure, you will most likely only see various birds, most of which will be Magpies. The tall grasses of the meadow give this trail an enchanted feel.

If you want to continue futher up to the Flatirons this trail provides access to Ski Jump Trail, Bluebell-Baird Trail and the Flatirons Loop Trail.

Length: 0.6 Miles
Ascent: 439'
Distance from Turing: 27 Miles/31 Minutes

Saint Mary's Glacier

St Marys Dr, Idaho Springs, CO 80452

The views at the top of this strenuous trail are well worth the high altitude hike. The trail goes from wide enough for a jeep to narrower with more numerous individual trails branching off through the woods. They all end up coming back together on the east side of Saint Mary's Lake. From there, take the bridge to cross the stream near the dam and continue up to the glacier.

It is about a mile hike from the lake to the glacier. From there, the trail continues approximately another mile up to a junction with Kingson Peak. Spectacular views of the area can be enjoyed along the whole journey, but the junction with the Kingston Peak Trail will provide the best photo ops.

Length: 1.9 Miles
Ascent: 1,214'
Distance from Turing: 46 Miles/56 Minutes

Lookout Mountain

987 Lookout Mountain Rd, Golden, CO 80401

Lookout Mountain Trail is a 7.7 mile moderately trafficked out and back trail located near Golden, Colorado that features beautiful wild flowers and is rated as moderate. The trail is primarily used for hiking and mountain biking and is best used from April until October. Dogs are also able to use this trail but must be kept on leash.

Sights along the trail include abundant wildflowers, grassy meadows, ponderosa pine forests, numerous birds, mule deer, and other wildlife. The flat-topped Lookout Mountain earns its name by providing views into the mouth of Clear Creek Canyon, vistas over Golden and Table Mountain, and a vantage to Downtown Denver. Rocky Mountain Peaks are visible to the northwest and southwest. Length: 7.7 Miles
Ascent: 1,525'
Distance from Turing: 21 Miles/28 Minutes

Red Rocks Trail

18300 W Alameda Pkwy, Morrison, CO 80465

The Red Rocks Trail at Red Rocks park gives you a chance to wander through the meadows just east of the imposing red monoliths that this Denver Mountain Park is named after. Close to Denver, the Red Rocks Trail is great for a sunrise hike, an inspiring trail run or a challenging bike ride. There's more: a cave carved into the sandstone a great geological overlook. Read our full trail profile below to get all the information you need on this Colorado hike: trail description, driving directions, Red Rocks Hiking Map, a video panorama of the park and more.

Overall this loop hike gives you great expansive views of Red Rocks, but if you want something closer in to the rock and shorter, then you should check out the Trading Post Trail in Red Rocks Park. Length: 3.5 Miles
Ascent: 523'
Distance from Turing: 17 Miles/24 Minutes

Flatirons Vista

3663 CO-93, Boulder, CO 80302

This trail is fairly straightforward - it's mostly flat and full of small, and sometimes loose rocks. At the end of the Doudy Draw trail, this trail begins mostly south through fairly open forest. You'll catch interesting viewpoints through the trees as you move along.

The trail loops around back to the east and eventually northeast all the way to the eastern end of Flatirons Vista North. From this high point, there are expansive views out over the plains. The option also exists to cross highway 93 here to meet up with the east Marshall Mesa trail system. Length: 3.5 Miles
Ascent: 285'
Distance from Turing: 24 Miles/29 Minutes
